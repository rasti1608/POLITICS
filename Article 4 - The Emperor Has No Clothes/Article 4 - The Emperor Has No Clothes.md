# April Fools' Day, 2026: The Night the Emperor Addressed the Nation

**IronRUST | Article 4 — The Emperor Has No Clothes**

---

On the evening of April 1st, 2026 — April Fools' Day — the President of the United States addressed the nation about a war he started 32 days earlier. What followed was 19 minutes of claims so detached from reality that the only remaining question is whether the joke was on us, on him, or on the concept of truth itself.

The markets answered immediately. Stock futures dropped. Oil jumped back above $100 a barrel. The world's financial systems listened to the Commander-in-Chief declare victory — and sold.

This article examines the speech line by line, separating the claims from the facts. Not opinions. Not spin. Just what was said versus what is actually happening on the ground, in the water, and in the wallets of 330 million Americans.

---

## 1. "Swift, Decisive, Overwhelming Victories"

The President opened with what may be the single most disconnected statement of his presidency:

> *"Never in the history of warfare has an enemy suffered such clear and devastating large-scale losses in a matter of weeks."*

Here is what "devastating losses" look like from the other side of the battlefield:

- **Iran struck Israel's Haifa oil refinery three times** in the past month — the third strike landing the same day as this speech.
- **Iran destroyed a Boeing E-3 Sentry AWACS** — a $270 million flying radar system — at Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia, injuring over a dozen U.S. soldiers.
- **Iran doubled its daily missile launches** compared to the first week of the war, according to the Wall Street Journal.
- **The Strait of Hormuz remains closed.** Twenty percent of the world's oil supply — blocked. Not by American forces. By Iran.
- **Yemen's Houthis entered the war**, firing ballistic missiles at Israel and threatening to close a second global chokepoint — Bab al-Mandab — through which 15% of global maritime trade passes.
- **Oil prices have nearly doubled** since the war began, from roughly $60 to over $100 a barrel.
- **13 American service members are dead.** Dozens more injured.

An enemy that is hitting your allies' refineries, destroying your radar planes, doubling its missile output, closing global shipping lanes, and watching new allies join its cause is not an enemy suffering "devastating losses." It is an enemy that is fighting back — effectively.

## 2. "Iran Refused to Negotiate"

The President repeated a claim that has become central to his justification for the war:

> *"The regime continued their relentless quest for nuclear weapons and rejected every attempt at an agreement."*

This is a direct inversion of the historical record.

**The facts, in chronological order:**

- In 2015, Iran signed the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) — a nuclear agreement verified by international inspectors. Iran was in compliance.
- In 2018, President Trump unilaterally withdrew from the JCPOA. Not Iran. The United States.
- In early 2026, the Trump administration engaged in negotiations with Iran through intermediaries. During these active diplomatic talks — on a Friday heading into a scheduled Monday continuation — the United States and Israel launched Operation Epic Fury on February 28th. The bombs fell in the middle of diplomacy.
- Iran's Foreign Minister has called Trump's repeated claims of ongoing negotiations "false and baseless."

**Former Congressman Joe Kent**, who served as Director of the National Counterterrorism Center before resigning in protest over this war, testified that Israel deliberately moved the goalposts — from "Iran cannot have a nuclear weapon" to "Iran cannot enrich uranium at any level" — specifically to prevent any deal from being reached.

**All 18 United States intelligence agencies** assessed that Iran was not building a nuclear weapon.

Iran did not refuse to negotiate. The terms were made impossible by design. The war was launched during negotiations. The President is telling the story backwards.

## 3. "This Short-Term Increase Has Been Entirely the Result of the Iranian Regime"

On gas prices, the President said:

> *"This short-term increase has been entirely the result of the Iranian regime launching deranged terror attacks against commercial oil tankers."*

This requires a timeline that apparently the President hopes Americans have forgotten:

- **February 27, 2026:** The Strait of Hormuz is open. Oil is approximately $60 a barrel. Gas prices are manageable.
- **February 28, 2026:** The United States and Israel bomb Iran.
- **Early March 2026:** Iran closes the Strait of Hormuz in retaliation.
- **April 1, 2026:** Oil is above $100. Gas is over $4 a gallon. The Strait remains closed.

The cause of rising gas prices is not "Iranian terror attacks." The cause is a war that the United States started. The Strait was open before the first bomb fell. Iran closed it because it was attacked. Calling the consequence of your own action the enemy's fault is not leadership. It is gaslighting — both literally and figuratively.

## 4. "We Don't Need Their Oil"

The President emphasized American energy independence:

> *"The United States imports almost no oil through the Hormuz Strait and won't be taking any in the future. We don't need it."*

This statement is technically narrow and strategically meaningless. Oil is a **global commodity**. It does not matter whether American tankers pass through Hormuz. When 20% of global supply is disrupted, the price rises **everywhere** — including at every gas station in New Jersey, Texas, and California.

American oil producers sell at global prices. When Brent crude hits $112, American crude sells at $112 too. The companies that produce American oil are not offering patriotic discounts. They are making record profits from a war that eliminated their competition.

The President told other countries to "buy from the United States." This is not generosity. It is war profiteering marketed as foreign policy.

## 5. "Build Up Some Delayed Courage — Go to the Strait and Just Take It"

In perhaps the most remarkable passage of the speech, the President told allied nations:

> *"Go to the strait and just take it, protect it, use it for yourselves. Iran has been essentially decimated. The hard part is done."*

The President is telling NATO allies — who were never consulted about this war, never agreed to participate, and never authorized the use of their bases — to now risk their own soldiers to reopen a waterway that was open 32 days ago, before the United States closed it by starting a war.

He is simultaneously threatening to withdraw from NATO because these same allies have not helped enough with a war they were never asked to join.

This is the diplomatic equivalent of burning down your neighbor's house and then demanding they thank you for the fire department's phone number.

## 6. The Fourth Deadline

The President announced another timeline:

> *"Over the next two to three weeks, we're going to hit them extremely hard... we're going to bring them back to the stone ages where they belong."*

This is the **fourth deadline extension** since the war began:

1. **Week 1:** "This will take 48 hours."
2. **Week 2:** "Five more days."
3. **Week 3:** "Ten additional days" (through April 6).
4. **April 1:** "Two to three more weeks."

Each deadline has been accompanied by the same claim: Iran is nearly defeated, victory is imminent, just a little more time. Each deadline has expired with the war expanding, not contracting.

This is the gambler's curse that Professor Robert Pape of the University of Chicago described: the belief that one more bet — just one more — will win it all back. Except the gambler is betting with other people's lives, other people's money, and other people's futures.

## 7. The Power Grid Threat

The President threatened:

> *"If there is no deal, we are going to hit each and every one of their electric generating plants very hard, and probably simultaneously."*

Professor Jeffrey Sachs, speaking on Judging Freedom, explained why this threat represents something qualitatively different from previous escalations:

This is not turning off a spigot, as happened in the 1970s oil crisis. This is the **physical destruction of civilian infrastructure** — power plants, refineries, pipelines, desalination facilities — in a desert region where millions depend on electricity for clean water, hospital operations, and basic survival.

The destruction of Iran's power grid would be **irreversible for years**, potentially decades. It would create a humanitarian catastrophe affecting 93 million people. And it would constitute collective punishment of a civilian population — which is a war crime under international law.

The President delivered this threat as casually as a business ultimatum. "Make a deal or we destroy your ability to sustain human life." This is not diplomacy. This is not strategy. This is something else entirely.

## 8. Venezuela: The Quiet Confession

Buried in the speech was a passage that received almost no attention:

> *"We are in a true sense joint venture partners [with Venezuela]. We're getting along incredibly well in the production and sale of massive amounts of oil and gas."*

The President is describing the seizure of a sovereign nation's natural resources as a "joint venture." The United States removed Venezuela's government by force, installed favorable leadership, and is now extracting oil and depositing the revenue in accounts "controlled by the United States Treasury."

When resource extraction from a conquered nation is described as a "joint venture," the word has lost all meaning. When the President uses Venezuela as a model of success while simultaneously bombing Iran, the pattern becomes clear: the United States is not spreading democracy. It is acquiring resources.

## 9. Opening with Artemis

The President opened his wartime address to the nation by congratulating NASA on the launch of Artemis 2. He spent the first minute of a speech about a war that has killed nearly 1,500 civilians and 13 American soldiers discussing a rocket going around the moon.

This is a pattern. Last week, he spent five minutes of a 22-minute press conference showing architectural blueprints for a White House ballroom with Corinthian columns. Tonight, it was astronauts.

When a wartime president opens a national address about an active conflict by talking about the moon, one of two things is true: either the war is not serious enough to demand his full attention, or he is not serious enough to give it.

- He did not mention the **82nd Airborne deployment** or the Marines arriving for potential ground operations.
- He did not mention the word **"Hormuz"** a single time in the context of a plan to reopen it.
- He did not explain how the war ends.

The absences tell the story the speech was designed to hide. The war is expanding, not contracting. New fronts are opening. The global economy is fracturing. And the Commander-in-Chief addressed the nation as if none of it is happening.
