# The Beast With Nuclear Teeth

*The Danger Nobody Will Name*

**By IronRUST** | April 10, 2026

---

Let me tell you how Nazi Germany was defeated.

It wasn't defeated by one country. It wasn't defeated by diplomacy, by sanctions, by sternly worded letters, or by hoping the ideology would moderate itself from within. It was defeated because the entire world — the United States, the Soviet Union, Britain, France, and dozens of other nations who agreed on almost nothing else — looked at what was happening and arrived at the same conclusion: this ideology cannot be negotiated with. It cannot be contained. It cannot be allowed to continue. It must be confronted by the unified force of every nation willing to stand against it.

And it worked. It cost seventy million lives. It flattened cities across three continents. It required the largest mobilization of human beings in history. But it worked — because the ideology of racial supremacy, of divine right to territory, of Lebensraum, of a chosen people destined to dominate all others, could not survive when the entire world said: no more.

Now I need you to hold that thought. Hold it carefully. Because what I am about to describe is the same structural situation — with one difference that changes everything.

## The Ideology

In 2016, an American journalist walked through Israeli cities with a camera. The responses from ordinary citizens — students, families, children — were uniform.

"Kill all Arabs." A child, laughing.

"If you gave me a button to erase Gaza — every single living being — I would press it in a second."

"Jews shouldn't marry Arabs. Jews are a special nation."

"Children grow up to be Arabs."

These were not fringe extremists. This was the center of Israeli society speaking without shame, without filter, with the casual certainty of people who have never been told that what they believe is monstrous — because in their world, it is not monstrous. It is normal. It is taught. It is law.

The Israeli Knesset has passed legislation allowing the execution of Palestinian prisoners — but not Israeli ones. The finance minister has announced annexation of southern Lebanon. The national security minister storms Al-Aqsa with armed escorts. Settlers burn Palestinian homes under military protection. The American ambassador to Israel publicly stated that Israel can take whatever land in the Middle East it wants. The prime minister, during Passover, compared himself to God visiting the ten plagues upon Egypt — including the killing of the firstborn — while bombs were falling on Iranian cities.

I need to be precise about what I am saying, because I know the accusation that follows. This is not about Judaism. This is not about Jewish people. There are Jewish voices — in Israel, in America, around the world — who are among the most courageous opponents of what is happening. Rabbis write to me. Jewish Americans fill my inbox saying: this is not us. This cult is claiming to be Jewish and they are not.

What I am describing is Zionism in its current, dominant, political form — an ideology of territorial supremacy that claims divine mandate for unlimited expansion, that has dehumanized an entire population through decades of law and propaganda, that frames every compromise as existential surrender, and that has consumed a society to the point where its population openly celebrates genocide.

If that description sounds familiar, it should.

## The Parallel

Every honest analyst who has examined this closely has arrived at the same comparison — the one that ends careers and invites destruction, but which must be stated because the alternative is silence in the face of the obvious.

Jeffrey Sachs: "We haven't heard this kind of language since Hitler."

John Mearsheimer: "A country that has no problem executing genocides."

Tucker Carlson: "The true death cult."

Alistair Crooke: "A large segment of Israeli society is not frightened of Armageddon but actually looking forward to it — because this is the path to redemption."

The structural parallels are not subtle. A state built on the principle that one group of people has divine right to a specific territory. A legal system that enshrines different rights for different ethnic groups — the textbook definition of apartheid. A military that has demonstrated zero restraint in the use of force against civilian populations — starving Gaza, flattening Lebanon, bombing Iran. A political class that frames every setback as existential and every negotiation as betrayal. An education system and media ecosystem that have produced a population where children laugh about killing and adults press imaginary buttons to erase millions. And an expansion doctrine — Greater Israel, from the river to the sea, and beyond — that, like Lebensraum, can never be satisfied, because the ideology requires perpetual growth. Stopping means the project was wrong. And the project cannot be wrong, because God said so.

This is fascism. Not the word thrown around loosely on social media. The real thing. The structural, ideological, societal thing. A supremacist state with a divine mandate that cannot stop because stopping invalidates the premise of its existence.

Nazi Germany could not stop at Austria. Could not stop at Czechoslovakia. Could not stop at Poland. Could not stop at France. Every annexation required the next one, because the ideology demanded it. Every conquered territory created new borders that needed to be "secured" by conquering the territory beyond them.

Israel could not stop at the 1948 borders. Could not stop at the 1967 borders. Could not stop at the settlements. Could not stop at Gaza. Could not stop at Lebanon. Could not stop at Syria. And now — Iran. The last major regional power that could challenge the project. The war that was supposed to be the final piece.

It didn't work. Iran survived. Iran emerged stronger. The Greater Israel project is failing. And that is where the parallel breaks — not because the ideology is different, but because the weapons are.

## The Difference That Changes Everything

Here is the thought I asked you to hold.

Nazi Germany did not have nuclear weapons.

When the world unified against fascism, the cost was staggering — seventy million dead, cities reduced to rubble, a continent shattered. But the world COULD unify. The Allies COULD invade Normandy. The Soviets COULD push from the East. The combined force of global civilization COULD physically confront and dismantle the fascist state, because the worst that fascist state could do — however terrible — was conventional. Bombs, tanks, armies. Devastating, but survivable for civilization as a whole.

Now imagine that Hitler had four hundred nuclear warheads.

Imagine that the Nazi doctrine included something called the Samson Option — the explicitly stated policy that if the Reich faces destruction, it will launch nuclear weapons at every major city in Europe, every capital of every nation that dared to confront it. Berlin goes down, but so does London, Moscow, Paris, New York. The world that defeated fascism is destroyed in the act of defeating it.

D-Day doesn't happen. The liberation of Europe doesn't happen. The Allies look at the cost — not millions of soldiers, but the end of civilization itself — and they hesitate. They negotiate. They appease. They watch the genocide continue because the alternative is worse. And the ideology — the supremacist, expansionist, genocidal ideology — survives. Not because it is right. Not because it is strong. But because it has made the cost of stopping it higher than the cost of letting it continue.

That is not a hypothetical. That is where we are right now.

## The Samson Option

Israel is widely understood to possess between eighty and four hundred nuclear warheads. It has never officially confirmed their existence — a policy of deliberate ambiguity that serves as both shield and threat. But their existence is acknowledged by every intelligence agency on the planet, and the doctrine governing their use has a name that tells you everything you need to know.

The Samson Option. Named for the biblical figure who, rather than accept defeat, pulled down the temple on himself and his enemies, killing everyone. The doctrine states, in essence: if Israel faces existential destruction, it will use nuclear weapons — not just against the immediate threat, but against anyone and everyone. The temple comes down on all of us.

This is not ancient history or theoretical doctrine. This is current policy, confirmed by Israeli officials, documented by journalists and academics, and understood by every government in the region. Seymour Hersh wrote the definitive account. Israeli military historian Martin van Creveld said it plainly: "We possess several hundred atomic warheads and rockets and can launch them at targets in all directions, perhaps even at Rome. Most European capitals are targets for our air force."

Even at Rome. Even at the capitals of countries that consider themselves allies. That is the nature of the Samson Option — it is not targeted at enemies alone. It is targeted at everyone. If we go down, everyone goes down.

Now place this doctrine inside the ideological framework I have described. A society where ordinary people say "give me the button." A government that quotes the Book of Joshua — the book of total war, where God commands the destruction of entire cities and the killing of every living thing within them. Ministers who have said on the record that they need "a big crisis or a major war" to complete their territorial project. A political and religious establishment where — as Crooke documented — a significant portion of the population does not fear Armageddon but welcomes it as the path to messianic redemption.

The Samson Option is not a deterrent in the traditional sense. A deterrent works because the people holding it are rational — they don't want to use it, and everyone knows they don't want to use it. Mutually assured destruction kept the Cold War cold because both sides wanted to survive.

But what happens when the people holding the weapons belong to a messianic movement that views destruction as redemption? What happens when pulling down the temple is not the worst-case scenario — but the fulfillment of prophecy?

## The Trap of Traps

This is the danger that nobody will name. Not because it is hidden, but because naming it requires confronting a reality so terrifying that the human mind recoils from it.

The world faces an ideology that — like Nazi fascism — cannot be negotiated with, cannot be contained, and will not stop on its own. Every historical precedent says the same thing: supremacist ideologies built on divine mandate do not moderate. They do not reform from within. They expand until they are stopped by external force, or they expand until they destroy themselves and everything around them.

The world defeated this ideology once before. It required global unity, unimaginable sacrifice, and the willingness to confront evil directly regardless of the cost.

But this time, the cost is not seventy million lives. This time, the cost could be civilization itself — because the ideology has nuclear weapons, and the doctrine that governs their use says: if we cannot have our divine kingdom, no one will have anything at all.

That is the trap. Not Trump's trap. Not Iran's trap. Not a geopolitical trap or an economic trap or a military trap. A civilizational trap — a situation in which an ideology that must be stopped has made the cost of stopping it potentially unsurvivable.

And every day that passes makes this trap tighter. Iran will have nuclear weapons within a year. Saudi Arabia will follow. Turkey will consider it. The Middle East will become the most nuclearized region on Earth. And Israel — watching its monopoly on nuclear power evaporate, watching its project fail, watching 500 million people who have witnessed its genocide on live television for two years begin to mobilize — will face the existential moment its ideology has always predicted. The moment when the enemies close in. The moment when the temple must come down.

The messianic wing will say: this is what we were born for.

The rational minority will say: we cannot do this.

And the world will watch — paralyzed by the same question that would have paralyzed the Allies if Hitler had the bomb: can we afford to stop them? Can we afford not to?

## What Must Happen

I am not a military strategist. I am not a senator or a diplomat. I am a man from a small village in eastern Slovakia who came to America thirty years ago because he believed in a promise, and who has spent the last three decades watching the machinery of power operate from close enough to understand how it works — and from far enough to see what those inside it cannot.

What I see is this: the world must do what it did in 1945. Not militarily — because that option may no longer exist without triggering the very catastrophe we are trying to prevent. But in every other way.

The ideology must be named. Clearly. Without euphemism. Without the fear of accusations designed to silence the conversation. Zionism in its current political form is a supremacist ideology that has produced apartheid, genocide, and the capture of the world's most powerful democracy. Saying this is not antisemitism. It is the observation of anyone with functioning eyes and the courage to use them.

The state must be isolated. Diplomatically, economically, culturally. The way apartheid South Africa was isolated. The way any state that practices systematic dehumanization of an ethnic group must be isolated by the community of nations if that community means anything at all. Every dollar of aid, every weapon shipment, every diplomatic cover provided to this state is complicity in what it does.

The nuclear question must be addressed. Not by threatening Iran — that has only accelerated the problem. But by creating a regional security architecture that includes Russia and China, that provides genuine guarantees to all parties, and that forces the question of Israel's undeclared arsenal into the open. A Middle East nuclear-free zone — proposed for decades, blocked every time by Israel and the United States — is not optional anymore. It is the only path that does not end in radioactive ash.

And the capture of American democracy must end. AIPAC, the donor networks, the dual-loyalty apparatus that Joe Kent described, the blackmail that Tucker Carlson documented, the institutional penetration that allows a foreign government to maintain offices inside the Pentagon and CIA — all of it must be dismantled. Not because Israel is an enemy, but because no foreign power — none — should have the ability to override the will of the American people and send their children to die for someone else's messianic fantasy.

These things are necessary. Whether they are possible is the question that will define the next decade of human history.
